# Product Differentiators — How TRD Compares

This document positions TRD against the alternatives a reader already knows. It is
organised by *model* — the recognisable category of approach — rather than by named
competitor. It is written to **build on what works and fix what doesn't**.

The "why" behind each difference is set out in the
[design decisions](./design_decisions.md); here we only draw the contrast.

| Compared to | The model | What TRD does differently |
|---|---|---|
| Traditional banks | The status quo that excludes this market | Channels global capital to the segment banks underserve, priced on verified performance |
| Marketplace lending platforms | Aggregated originators for retail investors | Global onchain capital; trust from verifiable asset performance, not a corporate audit |
| Smart-asset finance platforms | Productive-asset finance with asset monitoring | Independently verifiable data, not platform-curated; onchain capital; a debt facility, not revenue-share |
| First-wave on-chain RWA credit | RWA credit brought on-chain | Monitoring before capital; perfected security; real yield |
| The annual audit | Periodic, sampled diligence | Continuous, source-triangulated, independently verifiable |

---

## vs. traditional banks — the status quo

Banks in these markets largely do not serve the productive "missing middle":
lenders too big for microfinance and too small or unconventional for traditional
bank credit. Where they do lend, it is slow and collateral-heavy. TRD does not
compete with banks for their borrowers — it channels global capital to the lenders
the banks leave underfunded, priced on independently verified performance rather
than balance-sheet collateral.

## vs. marketplace lending platforms

Marketplace lending proved that aggregating loan originators for a base of retail
investors works at scale. That model is typically built on regional banking rails,
which exclude global investors and frontier markets; trust rests on the platform's
own audit of each originator's balance sheet (corporate trust); and the focus is
usually consumer loans with periodic reporting. TRD extends the same instinct — open
access to a loan book — but on **onchain rails for global capital**, with trust
resting on **independently verifiable asset performance** rather than a corporate
audit, and on **productive assets** monitored continuously.

## vs. smart-asset finance platforms *(closest category)*

The closest comparison is the category that already finances productive assets in
emerging markets with real-time asset and revenue monitoring and an investor-facing
platform. It validates the model. Two differences define TRD:

- **Verifiable, not curated.** In this category the investor-facing data is
  typically synthesised by the platform itself — portfolio-level, summarised, trust
  resting on its own underwriting. TRD publishes **attested, aggregated data openly
  on Walrus**, formed into KPIs in the front end, so investors **verify** the loan
  book rather than trust a platform's summary.
- **Onchain capital and a debt facility, not fiat notes and revenue-share.** This
  category typically funds through fixed-yield notes for accredited investors over a
  revenue-share base. TRD opens to **global onchain capital** through the
  **Open-Ended Adaptive Debt Facility**, secured by the **revolving receivables
  pool** — not a revenue-share structure.

## vs. first-wave on-chain RWA credit

The first wave brought real-world credit on-chain — a genuine step. Its documented
lessons are well understood: capital arrived ahead of monitoring, diligence leaned
on self-reported documents and social consensus, security was weak, yield leaned on
token incentives, and losses surfaced late. TRD builds the corrections in by design:
**monitoring before capital, independent and continuous verification, security
perfected over the receivables, and real yield from asset productivity.**

## vs. the traditional annual audit

The conventional audit is a once-a-year snapshot: an auditor samples a few loans,
trusts the rest, and sees borrower PII along the way. TRD replaces the snapshot with
a continuous one — independent sources triangulated, the detail kept private, only
the aggregate published and verifiable, and the KPIs formed in the front end. The
auditor stops being a person with a clipboard and becomes an always-on, attestable
process.
